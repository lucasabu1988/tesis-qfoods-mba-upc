## 1. Pecking Order Theory — Evidencia Empírica

### Prioridad Alta

- **Frank, M. & Goyal, V. (2020)**. *The Pecking Order Theory of Capital Structure: Where Do We Stand?*. SSRN.
  - URL: https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=3540610
  - Uso: Review exhaustivo del marco teórico; citable como sustento del Cap. I y II.

- **Silva et al. (2023)**. *Preferences, sources, and conditionals: a new approach to testing financing decisions*. Revista Contabilidade & Finanças, SciELO Brasil.
  - URL: https://www.scielo.br/j/rcf/a/3nRckmBzPYWHXgfRDT4VWtK/
  - Uso: 4,465 observaciones en empresas brasileñas; contexto latinoamericano directo.

- **Singh, K., Pillai, D. & Rastogi, S. (2025)**. *Pecking Order Theory of Capital Structure: Empirical Evidence for Listed SMEs in India*. Vision: The Journal of Business Perspective, SAGE.
  - URL: https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/09722629211057208
  - Uso: Panel data 82 SMEs en mercado emergente; metodología aplicable a QFoods.

### Prioridad Media

- **Sogorb-Mira, F. & López-Gracia, J. (2003)**. *Pecking Order versus Trade-Off: An Empirical Approach to the Small and Medium Enterprise Capital Structure*. IVIE Working Paper.
  - URL: https://www.ivie.es/downloads/docs/wpasec/wpasec-2003-09.pdf
  - Uso: 6,482 SMEs; contrasta Trade-off vs. Pecking Order directamente.

- **Nguyen et al. (2023)**. *Does COVID-19 affect small and medium enterprises' capital structure in Vietnam?*. Cogent Economics & Finance.
  - URL: https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/23322039.2023.2190268
  - Uso: GMM sobre cómo shocks externos alteran estructura de capital en SMEs.

- **Jansen, Michiels, Voordeckers & Steijvers (2022)**. *Family firm pecking order*. PMC.
  - Uso: ⚠️ Pendiente de búsqueda y verificación de URL. Relevante para Cap. V (Gobernanza familiar).

## 3. Altman Z-Score / Distress Financiero

### Prioridad Alta

- **Sutrisno et al. (2025)**. *Predicting financial distress in the food production sector*. DiSus / NASA ADS.
  - URL: https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/2025DiSus...6..731S/abstract
  - Uso: **Primera aplicación del Z''-Score EMS a empresas alimentarias no cotizadas**. Cita obligatoria para el Módulo F del SROD.
  - Fórmula EMS: `Z'' = 3.25 + 6.56X1 + 3.26X2 + 6.72X3 + 1.05X4`

- **Universidad de Karachi (s.f.)**. *A Comprehensive Review of the Altman Z-Score Model*.
  - URL: https://deanbs.uok.edu.in/Files/6d8be055-fc07-4110-8b8a-48477e9b960a/Journal/47e16e0b-c6cb-452f-8b45-71544cc457a4.pdf
  - Uso: Explica las tres versiones del modelo (Z, Z', Z'') y su aplicación en mercados emergentes.

- **Owner Journal (2022)**. *Altman Z"-Scores for financial distress predictions among food and beverage companies — Indonesia*.
  - URL: https://owner.polgan.ac.id/index.php/owner/article/view/696
  - Uso: 48 firm-year en sector alimentos; metodología replicable para QFoods.

## 5. Marcos Teóricos Complementarios

- **Robbins, D.K. & Pearce, J.A. (1992)**. *Turnaround: Retrenchment and Recovery*. Strategic Management Journal, Vol. 13(4), pp. 287–309.
  - URL: https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/Turnaround%3A-Retrenchment-and-recovery-Robbins-Pearce/14a76b67f2b92856ebe48185e8d474f80388f388
  - DOI: https://sms.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1002/smj.4250130404
  - Uso: Fundamento de Turnaround Theory para la introducción del Cap. IV. Modelo de dos etapas: retrenchment → recovery.

- **Richards, V.D. & Laughlin, E.J. (1980)**. *A Cash Conversion Cycle Approach to Liquidity Analysis*. Financial Management, Vol. 9, No. 1 (Spring 1980), pp. 32–38.
  - URL: https://ijirt.org/publishedpaper/IJIRT172180_PAPER.pdf
  - Uso: Working Capital Management para el Módulo E del SROD. Fórmula: `CCC = RCP + ICP - PDP`.

- **DiMaggio, P.J. & Powell, W.W. (1983)**. *The Iron Cage Revisited: Institutional Isomorphism and Collective Rationality in Organizations*. American Sociological Review, 48(2), 147–160.
  - URL: https://www.academia.edu/41967521/The_Iron_Cage_Revisited_Institutional_isomorphism_and_Collective_Rationality_in_Organizational_Fields
  - Uso: Teoría Institucional para el Cap. V de gobernanza. Tres mecanismos: isomorfismo coercitivo, mimético y normativo.

- **Silveira, R. et al. (2020)**. *Stewardship-oriented Culture and Family Firm Performance: A Study on the Moderating Effects in an Emerging Economy*. BAR – Brazilian Administration Review, Vol. 17, No. 2.
  - URL: https://www.scielo.br/j/bar/a/ryYBQqBJj99rKvgwgcwF9YR/
  - DOI: https://doi.org/10.1590/1807-7692bar2020180139
  - Uso: Stewardship Theory en empresas familiares latinoamericanas; contexto Brasil directo aplicable a QFoods.
